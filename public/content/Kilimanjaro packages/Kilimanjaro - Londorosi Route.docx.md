# Kilimanjaro Mountain Routes

The Londorosi Route

| Route summary   |                                       |
| :-------------- | :------------------------------------ |
| **Length:**     | 7–8 days                              |
| **Type:**       | Camping                               |
| **Difficulty:** | Moderate                              |
| **Best for:**   | Best overall (scenery + success rate) |

---

# **Day 1: Londorosi Gate to Mti Mkubwa Camp**

| Trekking time       | 3–4 hours                |
| :------------------ | :----------------------- |
| **Climatic zone**   | Rainforest               |
| **Altitude gained** | ~800 metres / 2,600 feet |

The journey begins with a drive to Londorosi Gate on the western side of the mountain. After registration, a short transfer takes climbers to the trailhead. The trek starts through dense rainforest with rich vegetation and wildlife, gradually ascending to Mti Mkubwa Camp (Big Tree Camp).

---

# **Day 2: Mti Mkubwa Camp to Shira 1 Camp**

| Trekking time       | 5–6 hours                |
| :------------------ | :----------------------- |
| **Climatic zone**   | Moorland                 |
| **Altitude gained** | ~600 metres / 2,000 feet |

Climbers leave the rainforest behind and enter moorland terrain. The trail crosses streams and open ridges before reaching the vast Shira Plateau. Camp is set at Shira 1, with first clear views of Kibo Peak.

---

# **Day 3: Shira 1 Camp to Shira 2 Camp**

| Trekking time       | 3–4 hours                |
| :------------------ | :----------------------- |
| **Climatic zone**   | Moorland                 |
| **Altitude gained** | ~300 metres / 1,000 feet |

A relatively short acclimatization day. Climbers trek across the Shira Plateau, one of the highest plateaus on Earth, with panoramic views of the mountain and surrounding landscapes.

---

# **Day 4: Shira 2 Camp to Barranco Camp via Lava Tower**

| Trekking time       | 6–8 hours                               |
| :------------------ | :-------------------------------------- |
| **Climatic zone**   | Semi-desert                             |
| **Altitude gained** | ~750 metres / 2,400 feet (then descend) |

This is a key acclimatization day. Climbers ascend to Lava Tower (~4,600m) before descending to Barranco Camp. Although you finish at a similar altitude, the time spent higher helps the body adapt.

---

# **Day 5: Barranco Camp to Karanga Camp**

| Trekking time       | 4–5 hours                |
| :------------------ | :----------------------- |
| **Climatic zone**   | Alpine desert            |
| **Altitude gained** | ~300 metres / 1,000 feet |

The day begins with the famous Barranco Wall climb — steep but non-technical. After crossing valleys and ridges, climbers reach Karanga Camp. This shorter day helps with acclimatization.

---

# **Day 6: Karanga Camp to Barafu Camp**

| Trekking time       | 4–5 hours                |
| :------------------ | :----------------------- |
| **Climatic zone**   | Alpine desert            |
| **Altitude gained** | ~600 metres / 2,000 feet |

A steady climb leads to Barafu Camp, the base camp for the summit attempt. Climbers rest, prepare gear, and sleep early before the midnight ascent.

---

# **Day 7: Barafu Camp to the Summit (Uhuru Peak) then down to Mweka Camp**

| Trekking time       | 12–15 hours                |
| :------------------ | :------------------------- |
| **Climatic zone**   | Arctic                     |
| **Altitude gained** | ~1,250 metres / 4,100 feet |

Climbers begin the summit push at midnight. The ascent is steep and physically demanding, passing Stella Point before reaching Uhuru Peak (5,895m). After reaching the summit, descent continues to Mweka Camp.

---

# **Day 8: Mweka Camp to Mweka Gate**

| Trekking time     | 3–4 hours  |
| :---------------- | :--------- |
| **Climatic zone** | Rainforest |

The final descent takes climbers through lush rainforest to Mweka Gate, where certificates are issued and transport is arranged back to the hotel.
